Garden Insect INFO Diatribe

The Cockroach

Synopsis

Nature’s most committed overachiever and least welcome roommate. A cockroach is essentially a tiny, armored survival expert who looked at every major extinction event in Earth’s history and said, “I’ll pencil that in and still make dinner plans.” While other species worry about climate, predators, or dignity, the cockroach has opted for a simpler lifestyle: exist everywhere, eat anything, and absolutely refuse to die.

The Praying-Mantis

Synopsis

At first glance, it appears to be a devout insect, constantly clasping its hands in prayer as if contemplating life's most profound questions. In reality, it is simply sizing up its next victim with unsettling intensity. What about the "prayer" pose? Less spiritual reflection and more "please come closer so I can grab and eat you." At the very least, they will control the other pests in the yard, I suppose.

The Butterfly

Synopsis

They begin life as a humble caterpillar whose main personality trait is “eat everything in sight.” Then, in a bold act of self-rebranding, it locks itself in a tiny room, dissolves into biological soup, and emerges as a delicate, airborne fashion model. Butterflies spend their days fluttering around like they have no responsibilities whatsoever, because, frankly, they don’t. Their entire schedule consists of sipping nectar and looking aesthetically pretty. Despite their elegance, they operate with the navigational confidence of a confused paper airplane.

The Wasp

Synopsis

The flying embodiment of bad intentions. Unlike bees, which at least contribute something to a garden environment with pollination, the wasp conversely wakes up every day and chooses violence towards anything that just happens to exist in the same space as them. Worse yet, it can sting you multiple times, because apparently one act of aggression simply isn’t enough to get its point across. Pair these with a group of mosquitoes and you got the true summer outdoor experience.

The Locust (Grasshopper)

Synopsis

They are like evil grasshoppers. On a normal day, it’s just a normal insect minding its business. But give it the right conditions, and suddenly it joins a massive swarm and unlocks its true passion: collective chaos. It's like, what if an insect learned the concept of networking—and used it exclusively for destruction (of any crop unfortunate enough to be in its way)? Hey, at least you know it's spring when they show up in droves, though.

The Spider

Synopsis

Nature’s eight-legged interior designer. Armed with nothing but silk and questionable intentions, the spider sets up elaborate traps just about everywhere they can reach. Every web is basically a tiny “live, laugh, lunge” sign for unsuspecting bugs and pests alike. Spiders spend most of their time sitting perfectly still, pretending to be decorative, until something moves—then suddenly they’re Olympic sprinters with too many knees. They are technically a net positive for the garden, though I wish they weren't so freaky looking.
